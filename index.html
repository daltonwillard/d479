About Taniti

Taniti is a small tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small active volcano. Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most of the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

Beautiful Beaches

Enjoy the pristine sandy beaches and crystal clear waters of Taniti. Perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and water sports.

Lush Rainforests

Explore the rich biodiversity of Taniti's rainforests. A paradise for nature lovers and adventurers.

Delicious Restaurants

Taniti currently has 10 restaurants: five serve mostly local fish and rice, three serve American-style meals, and two serve Pan-Asian cuisine.

Convenient Grocery Stores

Taniti has two supermarkets, two smaller grocery stores, and one convenience store that is open 24 hours a day.

Exciting Entertainment

Enjoy beaches, rainforests, and the volcano. Other activities include visiting a museum, fishing tours, snorkeling, zip-lining, pubs, dancing, movies, helicopter rides, arcades, art galleries, and bowling.

Sightseeing Adventures

Most tourists spend time in Taniti City, with native architecture and beaches. Other activities include boat/bus tours, rainforest hikes, or visits to the active volcano.

Easy Transportation

Most visitors arrive by air or a cruise ship. Taniti has a small airport for jets and propeller planes, and it's expanding to accommodate larger jets.

Ground Transportation

Public buses run in Taniti City, while private buses serve the rest of the island. Taxis, rental cars, and bikes are also available. The city is very walkable.
